  textEn: |
    Last weekend my sister Emma and I travelled to London. We planned the trip
    for many weeks, so we were very excited. On Saturday morning we got up early,
    packed our bags, and took the train from our small town. The train was fast
    and comfortable, and we arrived in the big city at ten o'clock. The weather
    was cold, and there was a little wind, but the sun was bright and the sky was
    blue. The streets were busy and full of people from many countries.

    First, we walked along the river. We saw Big Ben, the famous clock tower, and
    we took many photos. The clock was very tall and beautiful, and it rang while
    we watched. After that, we crossed a bridge and visited a big museum. We
    looked at old paintings and ancient statues for almost two hours. I really
    liked the Egyptian room, with its old golden masks. Emma liked the paintings
    of ships and the sea.

    At lunchtime, we were hungry and tired, so we found a small cafe near the
    museum. I ate fish and chips, my favourite food, and Emma had a chicken
    sandwich and a cup of tea. The food was delicious, and we rested for a while
    and talked about the morning.

    In the afternoon, we went shopping in a busy street. The shops were full of
    people. We bought some small gifts for our parents: a book for Dad and a
    scarf for Mum. We also bought postcards to remember the day. We felt tired,
    but we were very happy.

    In the evening, we were sad to leave, but we caught the train home. On the
    way back, we looked at our photos and laughed. It was a short trip, only one
    day, but I loved every minute of it. I want to go back to London again next
    year, and next time I want to stay for a whole week. There is so much to see
    in London, and one day is never enough for a city like this.
