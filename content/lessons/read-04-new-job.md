  textEn: |
    Tom started a new job last Monday. He works at a small company in the city
    centre, and now he is a junior designer. For many years he wanted this kind
    of job, so he was very happy when the company called him. But he was also
    very nervous on his first day. He did not sleep well the night before, and in
    the morning he checked his bag three times.

    Tom arrived at the office at nine o'clock. The building was tall and modern,
    with big windows. His new boss, Mrs Park, met him at the door. She was kind
    and friendly, and she smiled a lot. First, she showed him his desk and his new
    computer. Then she gave him a cup of coffee and introduced him to his
    colleagues. Everyone said hello and shook his hand. Tom felt better, and his
    fear slowly went away.

    Tom's first task was easy. He had to read some files and learn about the
    company's projects. He read carefully and asked questions when he did not
    understand. His colleagues were patient and helped him a lot. At lunchtime,
    the team went to a small restaurant together, and they told funny stories.
    Tom laughed and started to relax.

    After lunch, Tom helped a colleague named Sara with a poster for a new
    product. He chose the colours and the pictures, and Sara wrote the words.
    They worked well together, and the poster looked great. Tom felt proud
    because the work was interesting and useful.

    Now Tom likes his new job very much. The office is bright and quiet, and his
    team is friendly. Every day he learns something new about design. Next month,
    he wants to start a bigger and more difficult project. He hopes to become a
    good designer one day, and he is sure that this job is the right place to
    begin. In the evening, he called his family and told them all about his first
    day. "It was a great day," he said with a big smile. "I think I will be very
    happy here."
