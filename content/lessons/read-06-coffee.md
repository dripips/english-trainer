  textEn: |
    Coffee is one of the most popular drinks in the world today, but few people
    know its long and interesting story. According to a famous legend, coffee was
    first discovered in Ethiopia, in Africa, many centuries ago. A young goat
    farmer named Kaldi noticed that his goats became very active and full of
    energy after they ate the red berries from a certain bush. Kaldi was curious,
    so he tried the berries himself and felt the same energy.
    Soon people learned how to make a hot beverage from these berries, and the
    drink slowly spread to Arabia. By the fifteenth century, coffee was grown and
    sold across the Arab world. Busy ports helped the trade grow, and coffee
    became an important product. The first coffee houses opened in cities like
    Mecca and Istanbul, where people met to talk, share news, and play games.
    Later, traders brought coffee to Europe. At first, some people did not trust
    the strange dark drink, but it quickly became fashionable. Coffee houses
    opened in London, Paris, and Vienna. These places were so full of ideas and
    conversation that some were called "penny universities", because for the
    price of a coffee you could join clever discussions.
    Today coffee is grown in many warm countries and enjoyed everywhere. From a
    simple berry in Ethiopia, it has become a global drink that millions of people
    cannot imagine living without.

    Вскоре люди научились делать горячий напиток из этих ягод, и напиток
    постепенно распространился в Аравию. К пятнадцатому веку кофе выращивали и
    продавали по всему арабскому миру. Оживлённые порты помогали торговле расти,
    и кофе стал важным товаром. Первые кофейни открылись в таких городах, как
    Мекка и Стамбул, где люди встречались, чтобы поговорить, обменяться
    новостями и поиграть в игры.
