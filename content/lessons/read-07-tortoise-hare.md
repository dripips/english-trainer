  textEn: |
    A tortoise and a hare live in the same forest. The hare is very fast. He can
    run and jump. The tortoise is very slow. He walks step by step, all day long.

    Every day the hare laughs at the tortoise. "You are so slow!" he says. "You
    are slow, slow, slow! I am fast, fast, fast!" The tortoise is sad, but he
    says nothing.

    One day the tortoise has an idea. "Let us run a race," he says. The hare
    laughs and laughs. "A race? You and me? OK," he says. "I can win. This is
    easy!" All the animals come to watch. The fox says, "Ready, steady, go!"

    The race starts. The hare runs very, very fast. The tortoise walks slow, slow,
    slow. Soon the hare is far in front. He looks back. He cannot see the
    tortoise. "I am so fast," he thinks. "The tortoise is far away. I have a lot
    of time. I can sleep now."

    The hare sits under a big tree. He closes his eyes. Soon he is asleep. But the
    tortoise does not stop. He walks and walks and walks. He is slow, but he never
    stops. Step by step, he comes near the tree. Step by step, he passes the
    hare.

    The hare sleeps for a long time. Then he wakes up. He looks at the sun. "Oh
    no! It is late!" He jumps up and runs very fast to the end. But it is too
    late! The tortoise is there. The tortoise is at the end of the race.

    All the animals shout, "The tortoise wins! The tortoise wins!" The hare is
    very surprised and sad. The tortoise smiles. "Slow and steady wins the race,"
    he says.
