reading:
  textEn: |
    Once upon a time there were three little pigs. They were brothers, and they
    wanted to build new houses. So they said goodbye to their mother and went
    out into the world.

    The first little pig was lazy. He made a house of straw. It was fast and
    easy, and soon he sat down to rest. The second little pig made a house of
    sticks. It was also fast. But the third little pig worked all day. He made
    a house of bricks. It was slow and hard work, but his house was very strong.

    One day a big bad wolf came to the straw house. He was hungry. He knocked on
    the door and said, "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!"
    "No, no, not by the hair on my chin!" said the first pig.
    "Then I'll huff, and I'll puff, and I'll blow your house down!" said the
    wolf. He huffed and he puffed, and he blew the straw house down. But the
    little pig ran fast to his brother's house of sticks.

    The wolf came to the stick house. "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!"
    "No, no, not by the hair on my chin!" said the pigs.
    "Then I'll huff, and I'll puff, and I'll blow your house down!" He huffed
    and he puffed, and he blew the stick house down. The two pigs ran fast to
    the brick house.

    The wolf came to the brick house. "Little pig, little pig, let me come in!"
    "No, no, not by the hair on my chin!" said the pigs.
    "Then I'll huff, and I'll puff, and I'll blow your house down!" He huffed
    and he puffed, and he puffed and he huffed, but the brick house did not fall
    down. The wolf was very tired, so he went away. The three little pigs were
    happy and safe in the strong brick house.
