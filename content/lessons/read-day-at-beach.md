  textEn: |
    On a hot day in July, Emma and her friend Jack went to the beach. They had
    waited for this day all week, and they were very excited. Emma's mum drove
    them in the car, and they sang songs all the way. Jack carried a big bag with
    towels, water, and a small ball.

    When they got there, the sun was bright and the sea was calm. The beach was
    full of people, but they found a good spot near the water. They put down their
    towels on the soft, warm sand. "This is the best place," said Emma with a big
    smile.

    First, the children built a big sandcastle with three towers. Jack dug a deep
    hole around it, and they filled it with sea water to make a moat. Then they ran
    into the water and jumped over the small waves. The water was cold at first, but
    soon it felt nice and they laughed and splashed each other.

    After a while, Jack walked along the sand and found a pretty shell. It was pink
    and white, and it shone in the sun. He gave it to Emma as a present. "Thank you!
    I love it," she said, and she put it in her pocket.

    At noon, they were hungry, so they sat down and ate sandwiches and fruit. The
    sandwiches had cheese and tomato, and the fruit was sweet. After lunch, Emma's
    mum read a book under a big umbrella, while the children went to explore.

    The children looked for crabs between the rocks. The rocks were wet and slippery,
    so they walked carefully. They found one small crab, but it moved fast and walked
    back into the sea. "Goodbye, little crab!" Jack called.

    Later, an ice cream van came, and they could hear its happy music. They ran to
    it and bought two cold ice creams, one chocolate and one strawberry. They ate
    them quickly before they could melt in the sun.

    When the sky turned orange and the air grew cool, it was time to go home. The
    children were tired but very happy. Emma kept the shell to remember the wonderful
    day at the beach.
