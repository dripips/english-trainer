  textEn: |
    It is a hot summer day. The sun is high in the sky.
    A fox walks slowly in a big garden. She is very hungry.
    She did not eat anything in the morning, and now she wants food.
    She walks under the trees and looks here and there for something to eat.
    Then she looks up and sees grapes on a tree.
    The grapes are big and purple. They look fresh and juicy.
    "Grapes are good for a hungry fox," she thinks with a smile.
    "Oh, what nice grapes!" says the fox. "I want to eat them now."
    But the grapes are high, high on the tree.
    The fox jumps up. She cannot reach them.
    She jumps again and again. She runs fast and jumps high.
    But the grapes are too high for her.
    The fox sits down. She is tired and hot.
    She tries one more time. She runs and jumps with all her power.
    But again she cannot reach the grapes.
    Now the fox is very angry. She looks at the grapes one more time.
    "I do not want these grapes," she says. "They are not sweet.
    They are sour. They are bad grapes! I do not like sour grapes."
    Then the fox walks away with her nose in the air.
    She is still very hungry, but she says the grapes are bad.
    It is easy to say "I don't want it" when you cannot get it.
