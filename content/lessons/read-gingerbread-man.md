reading:
  textEn: |
    One day an old woman made a little gingerbread man.
    She made two eyes, a small nose, and a big happy mouth.
    Then she put him in the hot oven to bake.
    Soon the gingerbread man was ready. The old woman opened the oven.
    But the gingerbread man got up and jumped out. He ran away fast!
    "Run, run, as fast as you can! You can't catch me, I'm the Gingerbread Man!"
    The old woman ran after him. "Stop! Stop!" she said.
    But she could not catch him.
    The old man ran after him too. But he could not catch him.
    A cow ran after him. "Moo! Stop!" said the cow.
    But the cow could not catch him.
    A horse ran after him. But the horse could not catch him.
    The gingerbread man laughed and ran on.
    "Run, run, as fast as you can! You can't catch me, I'm the Gingerbread Man!"
    Then the gingerbread man came to a wide river.
    He could not swim. He was afraid of the cold water.
    A clever fox came and said, "Jump on my back! I can help you."
    The gingerbread man jumped on the fox's back, and the fox swam.
    "The water is deep now. Jump on my head!" said the fox.
    The gingerbread man jumped on the fox's head.
    Then — snap! The fox ate the gingerbread man.
    And that was the end of the Gingerbread Man.
