  textEn: |
    Once upon a time, a brother and a sister lived in a small house near a big,
    dark forest. Their names were Hansel and Gretel. Their family was very poor
    and had very little food. Often the children went to bed hungry.
    One morning their father said, "We must go into the forest to find berries
    and wood." So the family walked far into the trees. Hansel was a clever boy.
    He was afraid of getting lost, so he put small white stones on the path. "We
    can follow the stones back home later," he thought.
    But the children were tired, and soon they fell asleep under a tree. When they
    woke up, it was dark. They could not see the white stones anymore. "Don't be
    afraid," said Hansel to his sister. But they walked and walked, and they could
    not find the way. They were lost in the deep forest.
    The next morning, the children were very hungry. Then they saw something
    wonderful. There was a little house between the trees. The walls were made of
    soft bread, and the roof was made of sweet cake! The windows were made of
    sugar. "Look!" said Gretel. The children were so hungry that they ate some of
    the house.
    Suddenly the door opened, and an old woman came out. She smiled at them. "Come
    in, dear children," she said in a kind voice. But the old woman was not kind at
    all. She wanted to keep the children and never let them go. She locked Hansel
    in a small room.
    Gretel was very brave and clever. She watched the old woman carefully. One day,
    when the woman was not looking, Gretel found the big key. Quietly, she opened
    the door and helped her brother. "Run, Hansel, run!" she said.
    Hansel and Gretel ran out of the house and followed a little river back towards
    home. On the way, under a tall tree, they found a small box full of gold coins.
    They took the box and ran all the way home. Their father was so happy to see
    them again. He hugged them and cried with joy.
    With the gold coins, the family bought food and a warm bed. After that, they
    had enough to eat every day, and Hansel and Gretel were never hungry again.

    открыла дверь и помогла брату. «Беги, Гензель, беги!» — сказала она.
