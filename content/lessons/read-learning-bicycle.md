reading:
  textEn: |
    When I was seven years old, my dad bought me a shiny red bicycle for my
    birthday. It was the best present in the world, and I was so happy. But there
    was one big problem — I did not know how to ride it at all.
    On the first day, I wanted to learn by myself. I took the bicycle to the park
    and tried to ride alone. It was very hard. I fell down many times. My knees
    hurt, my hands hurt, and I wanted to cry. "I will never learn this," I said to
    myself. I was ready to give up.
    My dad saw me and came to help. "Don't worry," he said with a smile. "Everyone
    falls down at the beginning. You just need to practise." He held the back of
    the bicycle while I pushed the pedals. After that, we practised together every
    evening after dinner.
    At first, I was afraid to go fast. I thought I would fall again. My dad always
    ran behind me and held the seat, so I felt safe. "I've got you," he said. "Just
    keep pedalling and look forward, not down." Slowly, day by day, I got better.
    Then one warm evening, something amazing happened. My dad let go of the seat,
    but I did not know it. I was riding all by myself! I rode all the way across
    the park alone. When I looked back, my dad was far away. He was smiling and
    waving his hands at me. "You did it! You did it!" he shouted with joy.
    I was so proud of myself. I had finally learned to ride my bicycle. That night,
    I could not stop smiling.
    Now I ride my bicycle to school every single day. It is easy and I am not
    afraid anymore. Sometimes, when something feels too hard, I remember my dad's
    words: don't give up, just keep trying.

    Папа увидел меня и пришёл помочь. «Не волнуйся, — сказал он с улыбкой. — Все
    падают вначале. Просто нужно тренироваться». Он держал велосипед сзади, пока я
    крутил педали. После этого мы тренировались вместе каждый вечер после ужина.
    Сначала я боялся ехать быстро. Я думал, что снова упаду. Папа всегда бежал за
    мной и держал сиденье, поэтому я чувствовал себя спокойно. «Я держу тебя, —

    за днём, я становился лучше.
