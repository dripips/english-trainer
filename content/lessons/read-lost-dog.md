  textEn: |
    One rainy afternoon, Anna came home from school. As always, she ran to the
    garden to play with her dog, Biscuit. But Biscuit was not there. Anna looked
    everywhere — in the house, in the kitchen, under the table — but she could not
    find him. Then she saw that the garden gate was open. Biscuit was gone.
    Anna started to cry. Biscuit was her best friend, and she loved him very much.
    "What if he is lost? What if something bad happened?" she thought. Her mum came
    and gave her a big hug. "Don't worry," she said in a soft voice. "We will find
    him. Let's go and look together."
    Anna and her mum put on their coats and went outside into the rain. They walked
    around the streets and called his name. "Biscuit! Biscuit! Where are you?" But
    there was no answer. They knocked on doors and asked their neighbours, but
    nobody had seen the little brown dog.
    Anna did not give up. At home, she made some posters with a photo of Biscuit
    and her phone number on them. Then she and her mum put the posters on the trees
    and walls near their house. "Somebody must see him," Anna said.
    That evening, while Anna was eating dinner, the phone rang. A kind old man lived
    two streets away. "Hello," he said. "I think I found your dog. He is in my
    garden. He was wet and cold, so I gave him some food and a warm blanket." Anna
    was so happy that she nearly dropped the phone.
    Anna and her mum ran all the way to the man's house. When the door opened,
    Biscuit jumped up and ran to Anna. He licked her face again and again. "Biscuit!
    You're safe!" Anna cried. "Thank you so much," she said to the kind old man. He
    smiled and gave Biscuit one more little treat.
    That night, the rain stopped at last. Biscuit slept next to Anna's bed, safe
    and warm. Anna held his paw and whispered, "I will never let the gate stay open
    again." And she never did.
