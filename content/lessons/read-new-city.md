  textEn: |
    Last summer my family moved to a new city because my mum got a new job. I knew
    it was good news for her, but I was not happy at all. I missed my old house,
    my old street, and my old friends very much. I did not want to say goodbye to
    them.
    Our new flat was nice and bright, but the city felt big and strange. I did not
    know anyone there. The first weeks felt very lonely. In the evenings, I sat by
    the window and thought about my old life. "I will never like this place," I
    told my mum.
    On my first day at the new school, everything was difficult. I did not know
    where the classrooms were, and I did not know any names. At lunchtime, I sat
    alone at a table in the corner. I felt shy and a little sad. I just wanted to
    go home.
    Then a girl with a friendly smile sat down next to me. "Hi, I'm Mia," she said.
    "Are you new here?" I said yes, and we started to talk. Soon I learned that Mia
    liked the same books and the same music as me. We talked and laughed for the
    whole lunch hour. For the first time, I did not feel alone.
    After school, Mia showed me around the city. We went to a small park with tall
    trees and then to a nice ice cream shop. Mia told me where the best places
    were — the library, the swimming pool, and the cake shop. She even helped me
    with my homework that afternoon.
    Day by day, things got better. Soon Mia and I became good friends. Every
    morning we walked to school together, and every afternoon we walked home. We
    shared our lunches and our secrets. The big, strange city slowly started to
    feel like home.
    Now I love my new city. It is not scary at all when you have a good friend
    beside you. Sometimes a new beginning is hard at first, but it can bring
    something even better than before.
