  textEn: |
    The North Wind and the Sun are friends. They live high in the sky. The North
    Wind is big and cold. The Sun is warm and bright. They are happy friends.
    But one day they have a problem. They begin to argue.
    "I am strong," says the North Wind. "I am very strong. I am stronger than you!"
    "No, no," says the Sun. "You are not stronger. I am stronger than you."
    "I am stronger!" says the Wind. "I am stronger!" says the Sun. They argue and
    argue. They cannot stop.
    Then they look down at the road. They see a man. The man walks slowly. He has a
    big warm coat. The coat is brown and thick.
    "Look at that man," says the Sun. "He has a coat. Who can take off his coat?
    The strong one wins. The strong one takes off the coat."
    "Good," says the North Wind. "I go first. I am strong."
    The North Wind blows. He blows cold and hard. He blows and blows. The man is
    cold. But the man does not take off his coat. He holds it tight. He holds it
    with two hands. The Wind is tired now. He stops.
    Then it is the Sun's turn. The Sun shines. The Sun shines warm and bright. The
    day is warm. The man is hot. He is very hot. He is too hot now!
    So the man takes off his coat. He sits down under a tree.
    "I win!" says the Sun. The Sun is happy.
    Soft and warm is better than hard and cold.
