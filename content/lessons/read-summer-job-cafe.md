  textEn: |
    Last summer I got my first job. I worked in a small café near my house.
    I was sixteen years old, and I wanted to earn some money for a new phone.
    My old phone was broken, and my parents said I had to pay for the new one myself.
    So one day I walked into the café and asked the manager for a job. To my surprise, she said yes.
    On my first morning, I woke up early and could not eat breakfast. I was very nervous.
    The manager, Carla, was a kind woman with a big smile. She showed me everything.
    She taught me how to make coffee, how to use the big machine, and where to keep the clean cups.
    "Don't be afraid," she said. "You will learn fast."
    At first, I made many mistakes. I gave the wrong drinks to the wrong people.
    One time I dropped a cup, and it broke on the floor into many little pieces.
    My face went red, and I wanted to run away. But Carla just smiled and helped me clean it.
    "Don't worry," she said. "Everybody breaks a cup. I broke five on my first day!"
    My job was to take orders and bring food and drinks to the tables.
    I also had to clean the tables after the customers left and wash the dirty dishes.
    The work was hard, and my feet hurt a lot at the end of every day.
    But I really liked talking to the customers and learning their names.
    One kind old lady came every morning at nine o'clock for a cup of tea and a piece of cake.
    She always sat by the window, and she always left me a little tip on the table.
    "Thank you, young man," she said with a smile. "You work very well."
    The days were long, but they passed quickly because I was always busy.
    After two months, the summer ended and it was time to go back to school.
    I felt happy and proud. I had my own money for a new phone, and I had made many new friends.
    Carla gave me a card that said, "Come back next summer!"
    I learned something important that summer: work is difficult, but it can also be fun.
