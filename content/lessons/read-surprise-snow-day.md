  textEn: |
    One cold winter morning, Sophie woke up and looked out of her window.
    She could not believe her eyes. The whole garden was white!
    Big soft snowflakes were falling slowly from the grey sky.
    The trees, the cars, and the roofs all wore a thick white blanket of snow.
    Sophie ran to her brother Tom's room and shouted, "Wake up! It snowed all night!"
    Tom jumped out of bed and looked outside. "Wow!" he said. "There is so much snow!"
    A few minutes later, their mum came in with good news.
    "The school is closed today," she said with a smile. "It is a snow day!"
    The children were so happy that they got dressed very quickly.
    They put on warm coats, woolly hats, and thick gloves before they went outside.
    The cold air felt fresh on their faces, and the snow made a soft sound under their boots.
    First, they made a big snowman in the middle of the garden.
    They gave it a carrot nose and two black stones for eyes.
    Sophie ran inside and brought her old red scarf for the snowman's neck.
    "Now he looks perfect!" she said happily.
    Then Tom made a soft snowball and threw it gently at his sister.
    Sophie laughed and threw one back, and soon a fun snowball fight began.
    They ran around the garden, laughing, until their cheeks turned bright pink.
    A neighbour's dog heard the noise and jumped over the fence to join them.
    He jumped happily in the deep snow and tried to catch the snowballs in his mouth.
    After an hour, the children were cold and wet, so they went inside to warm up.
    Mum had a surprise for them. She made hot chocolate with little white marshmallows on top.
    They took off their wet gloves and sat by the warm window with their hands around the cups.
    Outside, the snow was still falling softly, and everything looked quiet and beautiful.
    "I love snow days," said Tom. "We should have one every week!"
    Sophie smiled and said it was the best surprise of the whole winter.
